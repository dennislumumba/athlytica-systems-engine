Primary legal instruments used for Athlytica/Big Ice compliance analysis:
- Kenya Data Protection Act, No. 24 of 2019 ("DPA")
- Data Protection (General) Regulations, 2021
- Data Protection (Registration of Data Controllers and Data Processors) Regulations, 2021

Provisions most load-bearing for this venture (sports data infra processing minors' biometric
data, with cross-border academy/club/insurer/federation access):
- DPA Section 2 — definition of "sensitive personal data" (includes health status; biometric
  data is separately defined and also sensitive).
- DPA Section 25 — core processing principles (lawfulness, purpose limitation, minimization,
  storage limitation, integrity/confidentiality, accountability).
- DPA Section 30 — consent must be free, specific, informed, unambiguous.
- Sensitive data / children's data processing — additional safeguards required per the
  General Regulations 2021 (parental/guardian consent, age-verification, best-interests-of-
  child standard). This agent's determinations reference these regulations but did NOT verify
  exact regulation numbers against gazetted text — flag any public compliance claim for a
  qualified Kenyan advocate's sign-off before publication.
- DPA Section 48 — transfer of personal data outside Kenya requires an adequacy finding,
  appropriate safeguards (contractual clauses), or specific informed consent to the transfer;
  heightened scrutiny for sensitive/children's data transfers.
- ODPC (Office of the Data Protection Commissioner) registration — likely MANDATORY for
  Athlytica regardless of size threshold, because biometric data and children's data
  processing as a *core business activity* triggers registration under the Registration
  Regulations 2021, independent of data-subject-volume thresholds.

**Why this matters:** Athlytica's core product processes exactly the two categories (children's
data + biometric data) that Kenyan law treats as requiring registration and heightened
safeguards as a matter of core business model, not incidental collection — this is a structural
compliance cost center, not a one-time fix.

**How to apply:** treat this file as a starting map for future compliance passes, not a citable
legal citation on its own. Any external-facing legal claim (marketing copy, terms of service,
DPA/consent forms) needs confirmation from licensed Kenyan counsel against current gazetted
text, since section/regulation numbers here are from model recall and regulations can be
amended. Also relevant on the receiving-jurisdiction side for cross-border transfers: EU GDPR
Art. 44-49 (for European buying clubs/insurers), Nigeria NDPA 2023, South Africa POPIA — flag
multi-jurisdiction review if academies/federations in those markets come online.
